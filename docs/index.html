Indian Healthcare - Opportunity?

Indian healthcare market is worth around US$ 100 billion and is expected to grow to US$ 280 billion by 2020, a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 22.9 percent

Niti Aayog Healthcare Vission

The NITI Aayog has allocated USD 55 billion under the 12th Five-Year Plan to the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, which is about three times the actual expenditure under the 11th Five-Year Plan. The share of healthcare in total plan allocation is set to rise to 2.5 per cent of GDP in the 12th Plan from 0.9 per cent in the 11th Plan. The 12th plan focusses on providing universal healthcare, strengthening healthcare infrastructure, promoting R&D and enacting strong regulations for the healthcare sector.

Niti Aayog 3 Year Action Agenda

  • Besides reduction in Maternal Mortality Ratio, Infant Mortality, Reduction in TB, Malaria, Premature mortality from NCD thrust is to Reduce Out-of-Pocket Spending to 50% of the total health expenditure (2014 estimate: 62.4%)
  • Increase government expenditure to screening, active case detection and disease surveillance
  • Provide essential health package including, primary, secondary and selected tertiary services with co-payment from the non-poor
  • Electronic Health Records to be linked to National Identification Number
  • Quality certification of public hospitals up to the level of district hospitals
  • Functionalize 9 newly sanctioned AIIMS
  • Strategic purchasing - Setting up of autonomous structure at Central & State/district level as a Single payer deciding on services to purchase, from whom and what rates
